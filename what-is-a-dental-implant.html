What is a Dental Implant?

Dentist

What is a Dental Implant?

Dental implants are artificial screws made of titanium or zirconium oxide that connect the jawbone and a dental restoration like a crown or denture. They are also called 'endosseous' implants or fixtures. These implants are an artificial root for a tooth. Once implanted, the implant integrates with the surrounding bone like part of the body. When placing dental implants in an upper jaw, dental surgeons may perform bone grafting to fix the implant.

The Procedure

Usually, dental implant surgery can be performed in several stages. Sometimes, when the tooth has been damaged and is still in its place, the dentist may need to extract it at another stage.

To begin, the gum is opened up to expose the bone. Then, each dental implant is carefully placed into a socket drilled at the precise location of the intended tooth. After the implant site is inserted, the gum tissue around it is closed. Often the dental implant can be placed at the same time as removal of the tooth, all on the same day.

If a dental implant has a screw-thread on its outer surface it can be screwed into position and if it does not, it is usually tapped into place.

When installing a dental implant, the main goal is to make contact immediately with surrounding bone. This provides stability and is maintained over time.

In order to support replacement teeth, dental implants normally have some form of internal screw thread or post space that allows a variety of components to be fitted.

Once fitted, these components provide the foundation for long-term support of crowns, bridges or dentures.
